Source

All records come from the USASpending award data archive, per-agency ZIP files covering the fiscal years listed below. USASpending sources its contract data from FPDS (the Federal Procurement Data System). No API key is required and there is no rate limit; the only constraint is that USASpending will occasionally IP-block a fast scraper after ~50 downloads.

What is a termination?

Every modification to a federal contract carries an FPDS action_type_code. Three of those codes indicate the contract was terminated:

CodeReasonMeaning
F Terminate for Convenience The government ended the contract because it was in the government's interest. No contractor fault implied.
E Terminate for Default The contractor failed to perform. Used for commercial-items and simplified-acquisition contracts.
X Terminate for Cause The contractor failed to perform on a non-commercial contract. Similar to Default but in different FAR parts.

The pipeline scans every contract modification in the source data and keeps only rows with one of these three codes. Non-termination modifications (option exercises, funding changes, administrative corrections, etc.) are dropped at ingestion.

Aggregation

Contracts can have more than one termination modification (for partial terminations, or when a termination is rescinded and re-filed). Each contract is reduced to one row, keeping the most severe termination action; Default and Cause outrank Convenience. Within the same severity, the latest action_date wins.

Deobligated amount is the sum of federal_action_obligation across all termination modifications for the contract. It is typically negative because termination mods pull money back off the contract; the dashboard shows it as a positive dollar figure.

Caveats

  • FPDS is contractor- and contracting-officer-reported. Late or missing modifications will not appear until the next bulk archive refresh.
  • A termination "for convenience" does not imply anything went wrong — it can reflect a change in agency priorities, appropriations, or mission need.
  • total_obligated reflects the latest cumulative value on the contract at the moment of the termination modification. It may have been subsequently adjusted.
  • Grants, loans, and other non-contract awards are excluded. This dashboard only covers FPDS procurement contracts.
